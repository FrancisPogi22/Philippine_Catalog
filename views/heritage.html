Iconic Tourist Spots

Banaue Rice Terraces - Ifugao

The Banaue Rice Terraces are called the 8th Wonder of the Modern World by locals, these terraces are said to have been carved into the mountains and hillsides by the ancestors of the local indigenous Ifugao tribes, and are well over 2,000 years old. It’s also believed that these terraces were created with little or no machinery and were carved mostly by hand. They provide level steps in the sloped ground, allowing people to plant and cultivate rice. Witness the awe-inspiring beauty of the Banaue Rice Terraces, showcasing ancient agricultural ingenuity.

Siargao Island - Surigao del Norte

Siargao is an island of nine municipalities in the province of Surigao del Norte. It is a teardrop-shaped island located on the northeastern coast of Mindanao. Known as the “Surfing Capital of the Philippines”. Siargao is mainly responsible for introducing surfing to the country. Siargao offers everything from crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, coconut palm forests, and of course, world-class waves that make it a prime surfing destination.

Chocolate Hills - Bohol

Known for its uniquely shaped group of hills, Chocolate Hills offers an extraordinary landscape on the island of Bohol. With an estimated 1,268 to 1,776 individual mounds, these cone-shaped or dome-shaped hills are actually made of grass-covered limestone. These unique hills get their name from their brown color during the dry season, resembling rows of delectable chocolate treats. It's a natural wonder you have to see to believe, perfect for a scenic hike or simply soaking in the breathtaking views.

Intramuros – Manila

Intramuros, or the ‘Walled City’, is one of the oldest districts of Manila. Intramuros is home to the city's iconic and historic landmarks that have been built during the Spanish occupation in the Philippines. It was built by the Spaniards – more specifically by Miguel Lopez de Legaspi – and is bound on all sides by moats and thick, high walls, some over 6 meters high. Because of its old-world appeal, many locals and foreigners frequent Intramuros to revisit the past and see centuries-old establishments.
